About

How we decide

A simple filter keeps the work focused and finishable.

We choose projects based on clarity rather than scale.
Ideas are pursued only when they can be expressed with tight scope and a clear endpoint.
Constraints are used intentionally to force decisions, reduce noise, and keep momentum high.
Not every experiment is meant to grow, but every project is expected to reach a finished state.
Over time, patterns emerge and the strongest ideas are expanded, while others remain complete as they are.
Ship a complete thing
A small finished product beats a large unfinished idea.
Reduce noise early
Constraints make choices easier and outcomes clearer.
Earn expansion
Scale only after the core loop proves itself.
